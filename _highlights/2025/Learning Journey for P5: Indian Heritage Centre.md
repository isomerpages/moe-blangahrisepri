<p>On 4th March, the Primary 5 students embarked on an enriching learning
journey to the Indian Heritage Centre. This visit provided them with a
deeper understanding of the history and contributions of the Indian and
South Asian communities in the Southeast Asian region.</p>
<p>During the visit, students explored various exhibits showcasing the migration,
culture, and achievements of Indians in Singapore. Minh Anh from 5 Opal
shared that she particularly enjoyed learning about different spices and
took a liking to cloves due to their sweet fragrance. Additionally, she
found the traditional Indian clothing to be beautiful.</p>
<p>Arihant from 5 Topaz was intrigued by the contributions of Indians to
Singapore. He learnt about their role in shaping Singapore’s economy, including
the establishment of the country’s first bank.</p>
<p>Overall, the visit to the Indian Heritage Centre was an eye-opening experience
for the students. They gained valuable knowledge about the heritage, traditions,
and contributions of the Indian community, making this learning journey
both educational and enjoyable.</p>
