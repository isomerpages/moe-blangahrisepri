<p>Our students took part in GREENvaganza 2026, a vibrant school-wide initiative
that brought sustainability to life through meaningful and engaging experiences.
From the Sentosa Sustainability Travelling Exhibition to hands-on activities,
students explored real-world environmental issues and discovered practical
ways to care for our planet.</p>
<p>Learning came alive beyond the exhibition. On 21 April, student leaders
took the lead at a school assembly, delivering an engaging talk-cum-quiz
that sparked curiosity and deepened their peers’ understanding of sustainable
practices. The launch of REHUB Recycling Bins – in collaboration with CORA
Environment – added another exciting milestone. A short video, created
by the LLP team, guided students on how to recycle paper effectively using
the recycling bin at the school canteen.</p>
<p>&nbsp;
<br>Back in their classrooms, Form Teachers reinforced green habits by introducing
the Beverage Container Return Scheme (BCRS). This is a national initiative,
led by the National Environment Agency (NEA), helping students make sustainable
choices part of their daily routines.</p>
<p>Through GREENvaganza, students not only gained knowledge but also took
meaningful action. We are proud of their enthusiasm and initiative as they
demonstrated 21st Century Competencies such as critical thinking, collaboration,
communication and information literacy, growing into confident and responsible
stewards of the environment.</p>
